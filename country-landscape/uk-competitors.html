Top 7 competitors

United Kingdom · Themes matrix & per-player profile

Top 7 UK competitors with verified pricing, channel, and messaging. The UK is mostly outright-purchase + strong NHS/BHF/Resus Council trust signals. Defibshop is the only direct subscription/lease competitor at scale — and their model is daily-rate, 5-year, B2B-only. Everywhere else, Pulse4all's wedge is the same: monthly transparent price, no upfront, no lock-in, training in-bundle, replacement on rescue.

Country snapshot · For founders / partners who want the top-line
Mature voluntary market. Replacement wave 2026-2028. Defibshop is the only direct lease threat.
The UK AED market is ~£85-90M/yr, 110K+ devices on The Circuit, ~30K OHCAs/yr, 7.8% baseline survival but 57.1% with bystander AED shock (London Ambulance). HSE has no legal AED mandate — UK is voluntary, persuasion-led. DfE's 2022-2023 schools rollout (20K AEDs, £19M) hits pad/battery end-of-life from 2026 — that's the replacement wave. Among 10 named competitors, only defibshop publishes a true rental alternative (5-year, daily-rate, B2B-only). Tier-1 device brands (Stryker, WEL Medical, Schiller) sell devices not service. E-commerce players (Safelincs, Medisave, Defib Store) compete on transparent purchase, no recurring relationship. Pulse4all wedge: monthly transparent £, no upfront, 3-month minimum, training + replacement bundled, consumer-grade onboarding for SMEs and multi-site hospitality.
