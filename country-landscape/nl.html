Netherlands

Home market · Densest AED + responder network in Europe

Pulse4all's home country. The Netherlands is the only EU market with a fully integrated national citizen-responder system (HartslagNu) dispatched directly from 112 control rooms. 170,000+ burgerhulpverleners arrive 2.5 minutes faster than ambulances. OHCA survival reaches 25% nationally, 43% in regions with mature coverage — the highest in Europe. AED is infrastructure, not optional. Pulse4all's job here isn't awareness — it's converting that awareness into recurring revenue and defending against the next subscription entrant.

Section 01 · Market Analysis

Densest AED + responder network in Europe

The Netherlands is the only EU market where citizen response + AED registry + 112 dispatch are integrated end-to-end. HartslagNu calls 170K+ responders directly when a 112 call is logged within range of a registered AED. The cultural expectation is that every neighbourhood, sports club, and VvE has 24/7 access to one. This is why NL has Europe's best OHCA survival rates — and why home-market subscription competition will only intensify.

Regulatory & structural drivers

1
HartslagNu — integrated citizen-responder system
170K+ responders dispatched by 112
The only EU national system that calls citizen responders directly when 112 receives an OHCA in range of a registered AED. Every AED that wants to be called by the system must be registered.
2
6-Minuten Zone framework
AED reachable within 500m everywhere
Launched 2008 by SixCase, scaled by Hartstichting + Philips. Goal: every postcode has an AED reachable within 6 minutes so a responder can deliver a shock in time.
3
Hartstichting (Dutch Heart Foundation)
Public-awareness engine + BuurtAED
Drives the demand creation side. Co-funds BuurtAED.nl crowdfunding (with Philips) for neighbourhood AEDs. Hartstichting accreditation matters for training providers.
4
Reanimatiepartners network
1,000+ accredited training providers
NRR-accredited Reanimatiepartners run CPR/AED courses across NL. NRR published Richtlijnen Reanimatie 2025 in Nov 2025. Partner channel for subscription leads.
5
Municipal AED subsidies
Most gemeenten subsidise 50% of cabinet costs
Typical model: 50% of cabinet costs up to €275-€400, plus €45 contribution to a CPR course + €25/yr for 3 years refreshers. Headwind for subscription (favours outright purchase) unless reframed.
6
VvE legal quirk
Board can't vote; individual owners can
A VvE board cannot legally vote to install an AED (null decision under Dutch property law), but can grant permission for an individual owner or group to install one in shared space. Suits an individual-signed subscription product.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

12 competitors · No one matches the 3-month opt-out

NL is competitive but fragmented — no single player owns >15% share. Subscription/rental competitors (CardiAid, AED Solutions, ProCardio) all carry 1-6 year contractual lock-ins. Transactional webshops (AEDwinkel, AED-Partner, AEDmaster, AEDcompany) compete on price-per-device. The pure subscription wedge — short minimum term, B2C-friendly, all-in monthly — is owned by Pulse4all.

Strategic takeaways

1
3-month opt-out is the unique wedge
No NL competitor matches it. CardiAid (closest twin) locks 72 months. AED Solutions 4-8 years. ProCardio annual. Pulse4all owns the "cancel anytime" narrative — defend it aggressively.
2
Defend the home market
NL is Pulse4all's most mature market — deepest brand recognition + highest churn-resistance benchmark for the other 13 markets. Whoever launches the next NL subscription challenger will copy CardiAid + price-undercut. Lock long-term loyalty via service quality not price.
3
Reframe the subsidy headwind
Municipal subsidies historically favour outright purchase. Reframe as: "subscription payments can be paid from the subsidised cabinet line" — turn the headwind into a tailwind.
4
VvE / Vastgoed is structurally yours
The legal quirk — VvE board can't vote, but individual owners can — fits an individual-signed subscription perfectly. ProCardio owns Vastgoed/VvE today; this is the segment to take from them.
5
BuurtAED is a partnership, not a competitor
Hartstichting + Philips initiative. Pulse4all could fulfil BuurtAED neighbourhood projects on subscription, removing the crowdfunding barrier and accelerating community deployment.
6
"Continuïteitsgarantie" should be our language
AEDcompany uses this term (loaner AED on failure). Pulse4all already does this — make it explicit in the proposition. "We are the loaner. Every customer gets a fresh device on failure or deployment — included."
