Section 01 · Market Analysis

Saturation benchmark — replacement market, not greenfield

Denmark is one of the most mature AED markets per capita globally. Modelled at €35-40M/yr (above population-weighted share due to dense deployment, replacement cycle on >20K installed base, and TrygFonden's procurement scale). Net device count grew only 3-4% in 2023-2024 — market is approaching saturation. Heart Runner enrolment grew double-digit YoY. Pre-EMS defibrillation rate rose from 1.4% (2001) to 11% (2024) — Circulation 2024.

Regulatory drivers

1
Sundhedsstyrelsen
Clinical guidance + 1-1-2 dispatch integration
Danish Health Authority publishes clinical guidance and integrates AED registry with 1-1-2 emergency dispatch. No statutory placement mandate but de-facto national infrastructure since 2007.
2
Lægemiddelstyrelsen
EU MDR competent authority
Danish Medicines Agency. Standard EU MDR pathway for AED medical-device oversight.
3
TrygFonden — Heart Runner Network
150K+ volunteer responders
TrygFonden / Danish AED Network is not statutory, but the de-facto national infrastructure: operational governance of the public AED register since 2007, plus the Heart Runner (Hjerteløber) volunteer network exceeding 150,000 registered volunteers (TrygFonden 2024).

National foundations

Heart foundation
Hjerteforeningen
Danish Heart Foundation. Partner in CPR/AED education and policy. Member of European Heart Network. hjerteforeningen.dk
Network operator
TrygFonden / Danish AED Network
The infrastructure. Hjertestarter.dk registry operator. Subsidies for outdoor cabinets drove 65% 24/7 access (highest in Europe). Pulse4all should auto-register every unit + advocate for low-friction onboarding.
Direct competitor
Falck
Largest emergency services company in Denmark — also the dominant AED subscription brand. Brand trust is enormous (national emergency services). DKK 4,375-6,580/yr (~€49-74/mo) on 3-year lock-in. Pulse4all wins on flexibility + transparent pricing, not brand.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Falck dominates · clear flexibility wedge for Pulse4all

Subscription model presence: MODERATE. Falck dominates with annual subscription plans. Others offer leasing or service agreements layered on purchases. No strong "all-inclusive monthly subscription" competitor in Pulse4all's price range. Entry requires competing on value + simplicity, not brand recognition (Falck is the 800-pound gorilla).

Key takeaways

1
Saturation benchmark
Highest AED density per capita in Europe (~340/100k). 65% 24/7 access. Net device growth only 3-4% — this is a replacement market, not greenfield. TAM is real but not expanding.
2
Falck flexibility wedge
Falck at €49-74/mo on 3-year lock-in is the gorilla. Pulse4all wins on flexibility (cancel anytime after 3 months) and transparent low-band pricing (DKK 225 launch ~€30). Don't try to out-brand Falck; out-flexibility them.
3
Heart Runner network advantage
TrygFonden's 150K Heart Runner network surfaces only registered units. Pulse4all auto-registering every device into hjertestarter.dk = service layer that Falck doesn't lean on transparently.
4
DKK pegged to EUR — minimal FX risk
DKK is pegged to EUR; FX risk is minimal. Pricing in DKK lands cleanly without hedging cost. Operationally one of the simplest non-EUR markets in the rollout.
