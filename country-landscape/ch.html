Section 01 · Market Analysis

Premium market · CHF 2,500-5,000 unit pricing pulls market value up

Switzerland is named in the Data Bridge Europe AED market report as a top-tier country. Estimated €55-70M/yr (above population weight due to high price premiums, above-average device density, and replacement cycle on legacy installed base). Swiss Heart Foundation cites typical AED unit cost CHF 2,500-5,000 — premium pricing pulls market value up. ~5-6% YoY growth; mature market with steady canton-led procurement.

Regulatory drivers

1
Swissmedic
Therapeutic Products authority
Swiss Agency for Therapeutic Products. Competent authority for AED medical devices under MepV (Medizinprodukteverordnung).
2
MepV (SR 812.213)
Aligned with EU MDR post-2021
Swiss medical device ordinance, aligned with EU MDR post-2021. Standard regulatory pathway. CH is not in EU but the medical-device family is the same.
3
Kantonale First-Responder-Verordnungen
Cantonal first-responder regulations
Structures vary canton by canton (Aargau, Zentralschweiz, Ticino each operate independently). No federal AED placement mandate. Cantonal procurement drives municipal deployment.

National foundations

Heart foundation
Schweizerische Herzstiftung / Swiss Heart Foundation
Established 1967. Sole non-profit cardiovascular foundation in Switzerland. Runs AED awareness and First-Responder education. Highest-leverage partnership target. swissheart.ch
Voluntary registries
Defikarte.ch & Herzsicher.ch
Two principal voluntary AED registries. Cantons of Ticino, Zürich, Bern lead in registration completeness. Auto-registering Pulse4all units in both = service-level differentiator.
Workplace context
SUVA — Defibrillator-Empfehlung
SUVA (occupational accident insurance) publishes AED purchase + deployment recommendations for employers. Channel into workplace deployment without statutory mandate.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Moderate subscription · opaque pricing is the wedge

Subscription model presence: MODERATE. Rettungskette and resQshock offer genuine subscription/rental models, but pricing is opaque (quote-based). No transparent, online-first monthly subscription exists. Pulse4all could differentiate with transparent CHF monthly pricing. Three language zones (DE/FR/IT) add complexity but are operational cost, not market access risk.

Key takeaways

1
Premium positioning supportable
Highest purchasing power in EU. CHF 2,500-5,000 retail AED price means competitor subscriptions also command premium (CHF 50-80/mo at Rettungskette). Pulse4all could price 30-50% above the EUR floor without losing positioning.
2
Pricing transparency is the wedge
All meaningful subscription competitors are quote-based. A transparent online CHF price + cancel-anytime delivers the same kind of trust differentiator that worked in NL/UK/DE.
3
Easiest content bridge
DE 63% / FR 23% / IT 8% — all three languages already exist as Pulse4all assets (DE from Germany, FR from France, IT from Italy Wave 5). Lowest incremental localisation cost of any new market.
4
CHF currency, post-July review timing
CHF (not EUR) — minor FX exposure but stable. Wave 5 launch after the end-of-July portfolio review = lowest-risk market in Wave 5. Quote prices in CHF, settle quarterly.
