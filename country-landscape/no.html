Section 01 · Market Analysis

Strongest demand signal in Europe — but registry incomplete

Norway is reported under "Rest of Europe" in syndicated reports. Modelled share with above-average per-capita spend yields €22-26M/yr. Norsk Hjertestarterregister (operated by NAKOS, launched April 2017) reported 6,550 registered AEDs in 2018 and acknowledges total deployed exceeds 10,000 (~4K unregistered). LHL Førstehjelp + TrygFonden-style first-responder schemes (NAAlarm / 113-app integration) drive registration completeness more than raw deployment growth.

Regulatory drivers

1
Helsedirektoratet
Operates national AED register via NAKOS
Norwegian Directorate of Health assigned NAKOS to operate the national AED register since 2015. No mandatory placement law; voluntary registration.
2
DMP — Direktoratet for medisinske produkter
EU MDR competent authority (EEA)
Statens legemiddelverk / DMP is the competent authority for AED medical-device oversight under EU MDR. Norway is EEA — same regulatory family as EU 27.
3
Schools CPR mandate (since 1961)
90% CPR penetration · strongest demand floor in EU
CPR training has been mandatory in Norwegian schools since 1961. ~90% of adults have had formal CPR training — deepest awareness baseline in Europe. Direct lift on AED bystander-use rates.

National foundations

Highest-leverage partner
LHL — Landsforeningen for hjerte- og lungesyke
52,000 members. Already sells AEDs via LHL Førstehjelp shop. Direct partnership = instant credibility + member channel. Highest-priority partnership of all 7 expansion markets. lhl.no
Heart foundation
Nasjonalforeningen for folkehelsen
Norway's national heart and dementia charity. Runs "Hjertestarter for alle" awareness. Public-health authority on cardiac issues. nasjonalforeningen.no
Registry & dispatch
NAKOS · 113-appen
NAKOS operates Hjertestarterregisteret (113.no). The 113 emergency-dispatch app surfaces only registered, accessibility-tagged units — registration tooling is a service-level wedge.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Subscription model underdeveloped · Folkestarteren is the only direct competitor

Subscription model presence: LIMITED. Only Folkestarteren offers a true all-inclusive subscription at NOK 500-800/mo (~€43-69). Most competitors are purchase + optional service. Pulse4all's NOK 325 launch price (~€28) is well below Folkestarteren — significant price-share opportunity.

Key takeaways

1
Strongest demand signal in EU
90% CPR training penetration (mandatory in schools since 1961). Bystander-use baseline is structurally higher than any other expansion market.
2
LHL partnership = unlock
52K members + already sells AEDs via Førstehjelp. Direct partnership delivers credibility + sales channel + registry integration in one move. Highest-priority partnership of the 7 expansion markets.
3
Pricing room below Folkestarteren
Folkestarteren at NOK 590/mo is the only direct subscription. Pulse4all's NOK 325 launch (~€28) is ~45% cheaper — clean share play with no upfront commitment.
4
Registration tooling matters
~4K of 10K deployed AEDs are unregistered. The 113-app surfaces only registered units. Auto-registering every Pulse4all unit in NAKOS = retention story + registry coverage gain.
