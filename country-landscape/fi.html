Section 01 · Market Analysis

Small but high-fit · digital-native subscription culture

Finland is grouped under "Rest of Europe." Modelled share based on healthcare spend (~1.4% of EU AED base) yields €16-19M/yr. Per-capita medical-device spend is above-average, but population is small (5.6M). Defi.fi (Finnish Resuscitation Council registry) lists publicly registered AEDs; estimated total deployed base 6,000-8,000. Adoption is voluntary; coverage of the deployed base is partial. Growth modulated by concentrated population (Helsinki / Tampere / Turku metro).

Regulatory drivers

1
Fimea
EU MDR competent authority
Finnish Medicines Agency (Lääkealan turvallisuus- ja kehittämiskeskus). Competent authority for AED medical devices under EU MDR. Standard EU pathway.
2
STM — Sosiaali- ja terveysministeriö
Emergency-care policy framework
Ministry of Social Affairs and Health sets emergency-care policy framework. No mandatory AED placement law nationally.
3
Finnish Resuscitation Council
Suomen Elvytysneuvosto guidelines
Clinical AED standards aligned with European Resuscitation Council. Operates the voluntary Defi.fi registry.

National foundations

Heart association
Sydänliitto (Finnish Heart Association)
~60,000 personal members · 15 heart districts · 200 local associations. Partner of European Heart Network. Runs CPR/AED education campaigns. Highest-leverage partnership target. sydan.fi
Clinical body
Finnish Cardiac Society
ESC member. Clinical authority on cardiac care. Useful reference for institutional buyers.
Registry
Defi.fi — voluntary AED register
Operated by Suomen Elvytysneuvosto. Voluntary adoption — partial coverage of the 6-8K deployed base. Auto-registering Pulse4all units = registry coverage gain.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Whitespace · no transparent monthly subscription player exists

Subscription model presence: LOW. No true all-inclusive monthly subscription model identified. Market is dominated by purchase + optional leasing through third-party finance. SafeAid and Presto come closest but require direct quotes. Finland represents a genuine whitespace opportunity for Pulse4all's transparent monthly subscription.

Key takeaways

1
Whitespace for transparent subscription
Market lacks a simple, transparent, all-inclusive monthly offer. Pulse4all's published pricing + cancel-anytime stands out cleanly against quote-based incumbents.
2
DESI + Klarna home market
Subscription-native culture. Digital-first checkout + email + Klarna familiarity = lowest-friction onboarding of any expansion market.
3
Swedish bridge for the 6%
~6% native Swedish-speakers can be served by SE Swedish content. Reduces incremental localisation cost — Finnish remains the long pole but Swedish covers a meaningful slice.
4
Tiny absolute ceiling
~5.6M population, ~6-8K AEDs deployed. Even at 11.4% peak share, ceiling is structurally small. Right-sized expectations matter — this is a clean field, not a volume play.
