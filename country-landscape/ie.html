Section 01 · Market Analysis

UK-adjacent market with developed subscription competition

Ireland is grouped under "Rest of Europe" in syndicated AED reports. Modelled share based on healthcare spend (~1.5% of EU27) yields €17-20M/yr AED market. Estimates from National Ambulance Service and Community First Responders put deployed AEDs at ~9-10K, but only ~1.8K are registered with NAS as of 2019 disclosures. PHECC National Defibrillator Register exists but adoption is voluntary — registration tooling is itself a wedge.

Regulatory drivers

1
PHECC — Pre-Hospital Emergency Care Council
National clinical practice standards
Sets clinical practice guidelines including the 2008 National Pre-Hospital Standards covering AEDs. Operates the National Defibrillator Register — voluntary adoption.
2
HPRA — Health Products Regulatory Authority
EU MDR competent authority
Competent authority under EU MDR for AED medical-device approval. Standard EU MDR pathway — same as UK pre-Brexit familiarity.
3
Sláintecare framework
Community-resilience funding
Sláintecare community-resilience funding lines drive ~7-8% YoY device growth — slightly above European average. No statutory AED placement mandate.

National foundations

Heart foundation
Irish Heart Foundation
Runs Heartsaver CPR/AED courses and community AED grants. Affiliated with European Heart Network. Highest-leverage partnership target. irishheart.ie
Ambulance service
National Ambulance Service (NAS)
Holds the registered AED count (~1,800). Pulse4all's value-add: auto-register every unit with NAS at point of sale — solves the registration gap visible to dispatch.
Community network
Community First Responders Ireland
Volunteer responder network. ~40% of registered AEDs are 24/7 accessible via outdoor cabinets — strong rural GAA-club deployment culture. cfr.ie

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Developed subscription market · defibshop.ie is the price-mirror

Subscription model presence: HIGH. Ireland has a well-developed AED rental/subscription market. defibshop.ie offers tiered subscription plans very similar to Pulse4all's model. Hearts.ie and Fleming Medical are strong local competitors. Differentiation key: Pulse4all's 3-month minimum (vs competitor 12-36 month lock-ins) is the wedge.

Key takeaways

1
defibshop.ie is the mirror
defibshop.ie's basic plan (€30/mo) directly mirrors Pulse4all pricing. Differentiation = flexibility (3-month minimum vs their 12+) + brand independence (they're a UK group offshoot).
2
Registration tooling is a wedge
~1.8K registered with NAS vs ~9-10K deployed. Pulse4all auto-registering every unit at point of sale solves the NAS gap — a service-level differentiator no current competitor leans on.
3
Irish Heart Foundation partnership
Highest-leverage local credibility play. Heartsaver CPR/AED courses + community AED grants align directly with Pulse4all's bundle. Worth a meeting before launch.
