United Kingdom

Live market · ~200 subs/mo current · 380 target

Largest live Pulse4all market. The UK has the most-developed AED awareness in Europe — every state-funded school has had one since July 2023 (DfE scheme, 20,000+ AEDs), The Circuit registry holds 110K+ devices, and London Ambulance reports 57.1% survival when a bystander AED shock is delivered. But the UK is also voluntary — HSE explicitly says no legal mandate. Replacement-cycle from 2022-23 mass purchases lands 2026-2028 — that's the play.

Section 01 · Market Analysis

Mature, voluntary, replacement-driven

The UK AED market is mostly outright purchase with strong NHS/BHF/Resus Council trust signals. The DfE schools scheme + Premier League fund + BHF Community Defib programme drove the 2022-23 sales wave. From 2026-2028 those devices hit pad/battery end-of-life — the replacement opportunity is in the tens of thousands.

Regulatory & structural drivers

1
DfE Schools Scheme — completed Jul 2023
20,000+ AEDs to 18,000 schools
£19M cost, every state-funded school in England equipped. Pad/battery end-of-life lands 2026-2028 — this is now a replacement market.
2
The Circuit (Resus Council UK)
National AED registry · 110K+ devices
Partnership of BHF, Resus Council UK, St John, NHS, AACE, Save a Life Cymru. Only 59% of registered AEDs are "always accessible." 24/7 access advocacy is a wedge.
3
HSE — no legal AED mandate
Voluntary, persuasion-led market
Workplaces, hospitality, retail have NO legal requirement to install AEDs (unlike FR/BE). This makes UK a sales-led, ROI-led, insurance-led market.
4
BHF + DHSC Community Funds
£1M DHSC + BHF 320-pack programme
Community AED Fund (£1M) reopened periodically; BHF Community Defib programme reopened Oct 2024 with 320 packages. Free replacement parts during lifetime — high-trust template Pulse4all should align with.
5
Premier League Defib Fund (closed)
2,014 grassroots football AEDs delivered
Jul 2021-2024 — all registered on The Circuit. Sets template; non-football sports (rugby, cricket, athletics, golf, gyms) are next.
6
Christian Eriksen / Damar Hamlin effect
Public awareness at all-time high
Post-Euro 2020 cardiac arrest moments + Oliver King Foundation campaigning have made AED a household word. Awareness is no longer the bottleneck — purchase friction is.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

10 named competitors · 1 direct lease threat

The UK competitive field is unusual: defibshop is the only direct subscription/lease competitor at scale, but its model uses 5-year lock-in and quotes in £/day not £/month. The Tier-1 device brands (Stryker / HeartSine / WEL Medical / Schiller) sell devices, not service. Pure-play e-commerce (Safelincs, Medisave, Defib Store) competes on transparent purchase price but offers no recurring relationship. Community Heartbeat Trust owns the parish/charity narrative.

Strategic takeaways

1
School replacement wave 2026-2028
~22K DfE-scheme schools whose 2022-2023 HeartSine/iPAD electrodes & batteries are expiring. Position swap-to-subscription as alternative to £400 pad+battery invoice. Frame as: "the AED bill you can budget for."
2
Defibshop = only direct lease threat
Their model is 5-year rental, daily-rate quotes (£0.98-£1.35+VAT/day), "REGISTERED COMPANIES ONLY" gating. Our wedge: consumer-grade onboarding, transparent £/mo, shorter commitment, B2C-friendly.
3
SME workplace = persuasion play
No legal mandate (unlike FR/BE). Decision is ROI + insurance + duty-of-care narrative. Subscription removes the £1,400 capex objection at the SME level.
4
Non-football sports clubs
Premier League Fund covered grassroots football only. Rugby, cricket, athletics, cycling, gyms, golf clubs still need a partner. Federation-led B2B with subsidised onboarding plays well.
5
~200 → 400 subs/mo path
School replacement (2026-2028) + SME workplace + multi-site hospitality + sports federations + Circuit-aligned community messaging. Brand permission already exists — execution is the gate.
6
Hospitality / retail multi-site
Hotels, supermarket chains, train stations — opex line fits better than capex. Single contract covering 50-500 sites is the highest-ROI growth lever.
