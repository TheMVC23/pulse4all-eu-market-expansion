Section 01 · Market Analysis

Strongest AED mandate in EU — but maintenance compliance lags

France has a paradox: largest installed base in Europe (~500K AEDs from the 2018 ERP mandate) but lowest registered density due to compliance gaps. The opportunity is not "more units" — it's subscription/maintenance/replacement of legacy units bought outright between 2019-2022 that are now hitting end-of-life electrodes/batteries. The Oct 2025 DGS bonnes pratiques note explicitly tightens this maintenance expectation.

Five regulatory drivers — France has the strongest AED legal stack in Europe

1
Décret 2018-1186 — ERP Mandate
Most extensive AED placement law in EU
Implementation of Loi 2018-527: all Établissements Recevant du Public (ERP) must install an AED. Phased: ERP 1-3 by 1 Jan 2020 · cat. 4 by 2021 · cat. 5 (incl. small venues) by 2022. ~400-500K ERP venues affected.
2
Géo'DAE — National Registration
Mandatory but compliance gap
All AED operators must declare devices in Géo'DAE (data.gouv.fr). Operated by Ministry of Health under AtlaSanté. Many ERP operators bought AEDs without registering — opens compliance + service opportunity.
3
DGS Note Oct 2025 — Maintenance
Direct subscription tailwind
Note d'information DGS/PP3/2025/121 (3 Oct 2025) tightens expectations on AED maintenance — electrode/battery replacement, periodic verification, signage. Direct tailwind for subscription with bundled maintenance.
4
Loi 2020-840 — Citoyen Sauveteur
Bystander legal protection + theft penalties
Creates "citoyen sauveteur" status: civil liability exemption for bystanders. Reinforces criminal penalties for AED theft/vandalism: 5 years imprisonment + €75,000 fine.
5
Loi 2023-451 — "Loi Influenceurs"
Constraint on B2C influencer marketing
Most restrictive medical-device marketing rules in EU. Promotion of AEDs by influencers strictly regulated. "Publicité"/"Collaboration commerciale" labels mandatory. Sanctions: 2 years + €300K. Implication: B2B/LinkedIn-led GTM is safer.

National foundations & public sector stakeholders

Highest-leverage partnership
Fédération Française de Cardiologie (FFC)
Founded 1964. ~27 regional cardiology associations + 259 "Coeur et Santé" clubs, 2,000+ volunteers. Funded by donations. Runs #SavezVousSauver campaign — "Appeler 15, Masser, Défibriller". Tier-1 public legitimacy. fedecardio.org
Training network
Croix-Rouge Française
Largest first-aid training operator. PSC1 program (mandatory school-leavers' first-aid certificate) explicitly includes AED training. Runs "Gestes qui Sauvent" 2-hour course + summer "Caravane d'été" tours. croix-rouge.fr
Government
DGS · AtlaSanté · ARS
Direction Générale de la Santé operates Géo'DAE. AtlaSanté runs the technical registry. Agences Régionales de Santé (ARS) implement regional AED policy — key buyers for collectivités. Plus AMF (Maires) and Assemblée des Départements as channels.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Competitive landscape — €29-54/mo subscription band, Lifeaz is the indigenous threat

Key takeaways — France competitive landscape

1
Replacement/migration play, not greenfield
~500K AEDs from 2019-2022 ERP wave hitting electrode/battery end-of-life. Oct 2025 DGS note tightens maintenance — direct tailwind for bundled subscription.
2
Lifeaz is the one to watch
French-made Clark device, retail via Boulanger 150+ stores, B2C+B2B subscription validated. Most direct competitor for Pulse4all positioning.
3
Price headroom at €29-54/mo
Pulse4all NL/UK/DE pricing (~€19-29/mo) lands at or below the floor of the French market. Margin opportunity even at launch-low €29.95/mo.
4
B2B GTM safer than B2C
Loi 2023-451 (Influenceurs) constrains B2C influencer marketing for medical devices. LinkedIn + trade press is the safer GTM path. FFC partnership = highest-leverage move.
