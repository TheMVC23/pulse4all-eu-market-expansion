Section 01 · Market Analysis

Standout growth · Legge 116/2021 unlocked the market

Italy is named among the top-5 country markets in Data Bridge's Europe AED Market report. ~10-12% YoY — highest growth rate in this 8-country set. Driven by Legge 116/2021 and Decreto Ministeriale 18 marzo 2023 implementation rollout (training, signage, registration mandates). No single national count is published, but regional 118 registries aggregate to a national estimate. Emilia-Romagna alone reports 8,385 AEDs in 2024 (+12% YoY from 7,464 in 2023) with a target of 10,000 by end-2024.

Regulatory drivers — Legge 116 transformed the market

1
DM 24 aprile 2013 ("Decreto Balduzzi")
Sports societies AED obligation
Original AED placement obligation for sports societies + training framework. The foundation that drove the 2010s deployment wave. Many of those AEDs are now hitting end-of-life electrodes/batteries.
2
Legge 4 agosto 2021, n. 116
Bystander use + mandatory 118 registration
Paradigm shift. Permits AED use by untrained bystanders in cardiac emergency (Good Samaritan). Mandates registration with the territorial 118 emergency dispatch. Single biggest market driver since 2013.
3
DM 18 marzo 2023
Implementing decree under L.116
Implementing decree under L.116/2021. Sets training curricula, registration data fields, accessibility hours. The operational tightening that's still rolling out across the 20 regions in 2024-2026.
4
Italian Resuscitation Council (IRC)
Clinical guidance, ERC-aligned
Sets clinical AED standards aligned with European Resuscitation Council. National authority on training curricula referenced in DM 18/3/2023.

National foundations

Heart foundation
Fondazione Italiana per il Cuore (FIPC)
Italian member of European Heart Network. Sister body Fondazione Italiana del Cuore runs awareness campaigns. Clinical body is Società Italiana di Cardiologia (SIC). fondazioneitalianacuore.it
Regional registries
118 — territorial emergency registries
L.116/2021 mandates registration with the territorial 118 emergency dispatch. ARES 118 (Lazio) and 118ER (Emilia-Romagna) are the most digitised. Registration tooling that integrates with all 20 regional 118 systems = service-level wedge.
Implementation references
EMD112 · Olympus archive
EMD112 publishes the most accessible regulatory implementation guides. Useful for compliance workstreams. Olympus archive at Università di Urbino is the reference for legal text.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Operational rental at €25-35/mo · 100% tax-deductible

Subscription model presence: MODERATE. Operational rental ("noleggio operativo") is an established financing model in Italy. EMD112 and Cardiosafe lead. Pricing starts lower than Pulse4all NL pricing (€25-35/mo vs €30-35/mo) but the Italian market requires local presence for training/compliance. The 100% tax deductibility on rental fees is a strong B2B sales hook.

Key takeaways

1
Highest growth in EU
~10-12% YoY post-Legge 116/2021. Largest absolute market among new entries (€100-125M, top-3 EU). Demand tailwind is structural, not cyclical.
2
Operational rental is the local model
"Noleggio operativo" at €25-35/mo is the established financing model. 100% tax-deductible — key B2B sales argument. Pulse4all should lead with the financial framing, not the all-in service framing.
3
Localisation is the long pole
No existing Italian-language asset. Content, ads, email flows all net-new at launch. The 6-week W4→W5 buffer protects translation throughput. Conservative 20 subs/mo at peak (2.7% share) reflects this lift.
4
CC could unlock more — review at M3
Digital adoption is lower than Northern EU. If M3 delivers >75% of forecast, adding an Italian CC agent at the per-market 3-month checkpoint is the logical next move. Conservative peak leaves significant 2-3× headroom from year 2 onward.
