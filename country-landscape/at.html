Section 01 · Market Analysis

German-satellite market — DE assets transfer, no native subscription leader

Austria is captured under "Rest of Europe" in syndicated AED reports — no AT-specific market data is published. Modelled share based on healthcare-spend (~2.3% of EU27) yields ~€27-30M/yr. Replacement cycle is starting to drive a refresh wave on devices placed during the 2015-2018 deployment surge. AED demand is increasingly policy-adjacent (workplace duty of care + driver's licence first-aid) rather than mandate-driven.

Regulatory drivers

1
AGES & BASG — medical device authority
EU MDR oversight
AGES (Agentur für Gesundheit und Ernährungssicherheit) handles medical device market surveillance under EU MDR (2017/745). BASG (Bundesamt für Sicherheit im Gesundheitswesen) is the competent authority for AED medical devices.
2
§ 95 StGB — Erste-Hilfe-Pflicht
Duty to render aid
Austrian Criminal Code obligation to render aid. No AED-specific national mandate, but "Wiener Definetz" (Vienna) and Österreichischer Rat für Wiederbelebung (ARC) guidance set de-facto standards.
3
Mandatory CPR for driver's licence
16-hour first aid · de-facto AED awareness
Mandatory 16-hour first-aid course as part of driver's licence curriculum creates strong B2C demand baseline — every adult driver passes through CPR/AED awareness once.

National foundations

Patient body
Österreichische Herzstiftung
Patient education, AED awareness campaigns. Sister body Österreichische Kardiologische Gesellschaft (ÖKG) owns clinical guidance. austrian-heart.at
Resuscitation council
ARC — Österreichischer Rat für Wiederbelebung
National resuscitation council. Operates Definetzwerk Österreich crowd-sourced AED registry (definetzwerk.at). De-facto standards body.
Vienna leadership
Wiener Definetz — Stadt Wien
Vienna leads Austria on outdoor-cabinet AED deployment since 2020. Several hundred outdoor cabinets installed. Strong municipal procurement model worth observing for replication in other Bundesländer.

Section 02 · Competitive Landscape

Fragmented market · WERO (Germany) is the only competitive subscription

Subscription model presence: LOW. WERO from Germany is the only provider with a clear long-term rental model at competitive pricing (~€30/mo). Most Austrian providers focus on purchase or short-term event rentals. Strong opportunity for Pulse4all leveraging German-language assets already developed for the DE market.

Key takeaways

1
DE asset transfer = launch fast
German language, content, legal framework, CC stack all transfer directly from Germany. Minimal net-new localization needed.
2
Fragmented field
Many small distributors, no dominant subscription player. WERO from DE is the only competitor at scale on subscription. Wide-open whitespace.
3
Driver's licence demand
Mandatory 16-hr first-aid for driver's licence creates a strong B2C demand signal — every adult driver passes through AED awareness. Sticky brand-build opportunity.
