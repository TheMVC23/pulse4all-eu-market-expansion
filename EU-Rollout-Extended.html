Pricing strategy — launch low, review at 3 months

Two parallel moves: (1) reopening markets (FR · BE · SE) drop from historic prices that were too high — biggest cut is FR, down €10/mo. (2) New markets (AT · IE · NO · DK · ES · IT) launch at low entry prices to grab share fast. FI + CH still TBD with subs team. Goal is speed-to-share, not margin optimisation — review each market's price at its 3-month checkpoint.

Launch schedule — 5 waves over 13 weeks

Wave 1 (FR/BE/SE) reopens on 11 May. Wave 2 (AT/IE) goes live 1 June; Wave 3 (NO/DK) on 15 June; Wave 4 (ES/FI) on 29 June; Wave 5 (Switzerland + Italy) on 10 August — six weeks after Wave 4, giving us time to digest the end-of-July portfolio review before committing the next launch. Digital-only markets — SE, NO, DK, FI, CH, IT — run without a call centre agent (automation + email carry conversion); Spain has a CC agent because of the price-sensitive buyer profile. Net CC cost vs an "all markets get CC" baseline: minus €25K/mo (six markets digital-only saves €30K, Spain CC adds €5K).

Review framework — when to scale, hold, or kill

Three review gates: per-market 3-month checkpoint at each market's own M3, an end-of-July portfolio review (Waves 1-4 have data, Wave 5 is the next decision), and a 15 September portfolio review (all five waves live, Wave 5 has ~5 weeks of M1 data).

Per-market 3-month checkpoint

For each market, at M3 post-launch we evaluate against the trajectory and decide: scale, hold, or kill.

Outcome at M3 Threshold Decision
Working >75% of M3 forecast subs hit · CPA inside band Add CC agent if currently digital-only — CC pays for itself at ~10 incremental subs/mo. Scale ad spend per plan. Queue for Wave 5+ expansion conversation.
Mixed 40-75% of M3 forecast · CPA somewhat above target Hold and adjust — creative iteration, new keywords, partnership outreach. Re-evaluate at M6. No new spend commitment until M6 read.
Failing <40% of M3 forecast · OR CPA stays above €450 Pause campaigns + 30-day fix window. If still failing at end of fix window, full pull-out — reclaim budget and rotate to higher-performing markets.

End-of-July portfolio review — Wave 5 go/no-go gate

By 31 July: Wave 1 is at M3, Waves 2/3 at M2, Wave 4 at M1. Wave 5 (CH + IT) is scheduled for 10 August — this review is the gate where we either confirm the launch or pull it.

Question How we answer it
Which countries are working?Apply per-market 3-month checkpoint to Wave 1; early-read indicators for Waves 2-4. Identify CC retrofit candidates.
Which need more localisation?Look at search query data, conversion-rate gaps vs. forecast, and CC call notes. Translation gaps surface here.
Stop candidates?Markets failing the M3 threshold get killed. Reclaim budget for top performers and Wave 5 prep.
Wave 5 confirm or pull?If portfolio July totals are below 60% of forecast OR Wave 1 markets are mostly failing at M3, pull Wave 5 by 1 August. If at-or-above forecast, confirm Italian translation throughput and proceed to 10 August launch.

15 September portfolio review — five waves live

By mid-September: all five waves live, Wave 5 (CH + IT) has ~5 weeks of M1 data. This is the first review where the entire 11-market portfolio is producing concurrent signal — and the right gate to decide on Wave 6+ from the future-markets list.

Question How we answer it
How is Wave 5 reading?CH should be at or above its forecast given easiest fit; IT will be slower-burn given full localization. Read both against M1 forecast (CH 6 subs, IT 5 subs).
Are M3 calls holding for Wave 1?Wave 1 is at M4 by mid-September — past the formal 3-month checkpoint. Confirm whether the post-checkpoint decisions (CC retrofits, kills, scale-ups) are paying back.
Is the digital-first model proving?Six markets now running digital-only (SE, NO, DK, FI, CH, IT). If the cohort is hitting forecast, the model is validated. If not, Wave 6 gets paused.
Wave 6 candidates?If 7+ markets are tracking ahead of forecast and digital-first is proving, queue Wave 6 from the future-markets table (Poland + Portugal first — biggest remaining addressable market).
Year-2 efficiency play?Enough data now to start year-2 thinking: which markets graduate from "frontload at €400 cap" to true efficiency optimization (lower CPO targets, smaller daily budgets).

If digital-first fails portfolio-wide

Defined as: portfolio July totals below 60% of forecast (i.e. below ~88 subs from expansion in July).

  • → Pause Wave 4 if not yet live (decision point: 25 June, four days before scheduled launch)
  • → Concentrate budget on top-2 performing markets — kill or cut budget on the rest
  • → Reactivate CC for markets where digital is structurally underperforming (CC retrofit ~€1,200/mo per market)
  • → Slow to a 6-week wave cadence instead of 2-week, to give translation + ops more breathing room
  • → Re-baseline forecast with the new model before any further expansion conversation

Key assumptions

These are the inputs that drive the forecast. If any assumption shifts, the numbers shift with it.

M1 at €400 CPO cap — deliberate pipeline fill Front-loaded ad spend in M1 to capture leads that convert in M2-M3. Observed M1 CPO is high by design; effective CPO across the M1→M3 cohort is much lower once leads finish converting.
Net CC cost: minus €15K/mo Four markets digital-only (SE, NO, DK, FI) avoid €5K/mo agent cost each = €20K/mo saved. Spain adds €5K/mo for a Spanish CC agent (price-sensitive market needs phone rescue). Net vs an "all markets get CC" baseline: -€15K/mo.
Shopify site uplift: ~20% New Shopify sites are ~25% better suited per market than the old WordPress sites — translating to ~20% CPO improvement (conservative ratio).
Entity trust boost: +15% Local entity in France, Belgium, and Sweden at launch removes the single biggest historical drag. Adds ~15% CPO improvement on top of Shopify boost.
CC languages — Spanish is the only new hire Shared with existing markets: DE (→AT), EN (→IE), FR+NL (→BE), FR (→FR). Spanish (→ES) is a new agent at ~€5K/mo — only incremental CC cost in the rollout. Finland skips CC entirely (digital-only).
Conservative peak — significant headroom Portfolio peak of 450 subs/mo = 7.8% of the 5,787/mo combined AED market across these 9 countries. Roughly half the 15% benchmark UK/NL/DE reached — leaves meaningful upside if the playbook proves out and budgets can expand.
Staggered waves protect focus 2-week gap between waves means the team can concentrate on one launch at a time, debug tracking/creative/CC training before moving to the next. No simultaneous 7-market launch chaos.
Translation is the binding capacity constraint Wave timing (3w · 2w · 2w intervals) is paced by translation throughput, not by ad readiness or CC training. Going faster requires additional translation capacity from an external agency — a one-off ~€2-4K bump per accelerated market.
Digital-first by default — CC retrofit if proven Default is digital-only (ads + email + automation) for new markets. If a market hits >75% of M3 forecast, we add a CC agent (€1,200/mo) to capture the next 25% of demand. If digital is structurally failing portfolio-wide, we slow the cadence and add CC earlier.
No partnerships in base case Forecast assumes no BHF-equivalent partnerships yet. Adding LHL (Norway), TrygFonden (Denmark), or IHF (Ireland) would accelerate beyond these numbers — especially valuable for digital-only markets where trust signals matter more.
