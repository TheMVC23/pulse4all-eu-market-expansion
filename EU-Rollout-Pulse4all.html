Portfolio kill triggers

If these conditions hit, pause and rebalance before scaling further. Protects against chasing bad spend.

  • → If blended CPO exceeds €320 in Month 1, pause new-market launches and concentrate budget on FR/BE/SE (where entity advantage is known)
  • → If any single market absorbs >35% of total weekly budget, diversify before scaling further — avoids over-concentration risk
  • → If M2 totals come in below 70% of forecast (below 320 subs), reassess the ~20% Shopify uplift assumption with actual CRO data before committing to M3 budget

Key assumptions

These are the inputs that drive the forecast. If any assumption shifts, the numbers shift with it.

Shopify site uplift: ~20% New Shopify sites are 25% better suited per market than the old WordPress sites — translating to approximately 20% CPO improvement (conservative ratio).
Entity trust boost: +15% Local entity in France, Belgium, and Sweden at launch removes the single biggest historical drag (no trust, no credibility). Adds ~15% CPO improvement on top of Shopify boost.
Call-centre languages already ready DE (→AT), EN (→IE), FR+NL (→BE), FR (→FR), SE (→SE). Newco needs to add Danish (DK) and Norwegian (NO) — ~€5K/mo fixed cost per new language.
Disciplined budget scaling Budget only scales in month N+1 if month N hit or beat target CPO. No blind increases. Max €25K per market where subscription volume supports it.
Max CPO cap: €400 Never exceed €400 per subscription acquisition in any market. This creates a natural ceiling — if CPO creeps above €400, we reduce spend rather than chase volume.
No partnerships in base case Forecast assumes no BHF-equivalent partnerships yet. Adding LHL (Norway), TrygFonden (Denmark), or IHF (Ireland) would accelerate further beyond these numbers.
