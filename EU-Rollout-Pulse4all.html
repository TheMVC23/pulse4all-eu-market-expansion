11 May to February 2027 — 9 markets, 450 subscriptions per month at peak

Staggered rollout in four waves over seven weeks. Wave 1 reopens FR/BE/SE on 11 May. Wave 2 launches Austria + Ireland on 1 June. Wave 3 takes Norway + Denmark live on 15 June. Wave 4 closes the rollout with Spain + Finland on 29 June. Month 1 is frontloaded at the €400 CPO cap to fill the pipeline — leads generated in M1 convert in M2 and M3 as learning phases exit and word-of-mouth kicks in.

Total monthly subscriptions — base + rollout

DE and NL provide a steady 150 subs/mo base today (DE 80, NL 70). Each wave layers on top from its launch month, ramping to its M8 peak. By February 2027, the four-wave rollout adds 450 subs/mo on top of the base — taking the portfolio from 150 to 600 subs/mo, a 4× lift on the current run rate.

Launch schedule — 4 waves over 7 weeks

Wave 1 (FR/BE/SE) reopens on 11 May. Three weeks later Wave 2 (AT/IE) goes live, then Wave 3 (NO/DK) two weeks after that, and Wave 4 (ES/FI) two weeks behind Wave 3. This staggers the team's attention so each launch gets a focused window. Digital-only markets — SE, NO, DK, Finland — run without a call centre agent (automation + email carry conversion); Spain has a CC agent because of the price-sensitive buyer profile. Net CC cost vs an "all markets get CC" baseline: minus €15K/mo (four markets digital-only saves €20K, Spain CC adds €5K).

Monthly ramp logic — why M1 is at the cap and M2/M3 accelerate

Each market follows this curve from its own launch date. Key assumptions

These are the inputs that drive the forecast. If any assumption shifts, the numbers shift with it.

M1 at €400 CPO cap — deliberate pipeline fill Front-loaded ad spend in M1 to capture leads that convert in M2-M3. Observed M1 CPO is high by design; effective CPO across the M1→M3 cohort is much lower once leads finish converting.
Net CC cost: minus €15K/mo Four markets digital-only (SE, NO, DK, FI) avoid €5K/mo agent cost each = €20K/mo saved. Spain adds €5K/mo for a Spanish CC agent (price-sensitive market needs phone rescue). Net vs an "all markets get CC" baseline: -€15K/mo.
Shopify site uplift: ~20% New Shopify sites are ~25% better suited per market than the old WordPress sites — translating to ~20% CPO improvement (conservative ratio).
Entity trust boost: +15% Local entity in France, Belgium, and Sweden at launch removes the single biggest historical drag. Adds ~15% CPO improvement on top of Shopify boost.
CC languages — Spanish is the only new hire Shared with existing markets: DE (→AT), EN (→IE), FR+NL (→BE), FR (→FR). Spanish (→ES) is a new agent at ~€5K/mo — only incremental CC cost in the rollout. Finland skips CC entirely (digital-only).
Conservative peak — significant headroom Portfolio peak of 450 subs/mo = 7.8% of the 5,787/mo combined AED market across these 9 countries. Roughly half the 15% benchmark UK/NL/DE reached — leaves meaningful upside if the playbook proves out and budgets can expand.
Staggered waves protect focus 2-week gap between waves means the team can concentrate on one launch at a time, debug tracking/creative/CC training before moving to the next. No simultaneous 7-market launch chaos.
No partnerships in base case Forecast assumes no BHF-equivalent partnerships yet. Adding LHL (Norway), TrygFonden (Denmark), or IHF (Ireland) would accelerate beyond these numbers — especially valuable for digital-only markets where trust signals matter more.
